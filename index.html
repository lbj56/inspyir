Who

Eric Krebs founded Inspyir on the belief that a company should be equal parts customer centric and employee focused. This leadership philosophy has guided Eric through his three decade career in the military and corporate world. Eric served in the U.S. Navy on numerous worldwide deployments as a P-3 Tactical Coordinator and Mission Commander. Eric served in numerous leadership roles, culminating in his selection as a P-3C Squadron Officer in Charge. Later, he was handpicked to serve as the Executive Assistant to the Chief of Navy Reserve. After Eric retired from the Navy he was recruited by a premier Cyber Security firm to become its VP of Strategy. His focus was on long-range cyber security efforts across both the Federal government and the private sector.

Eric received his B.S. in Systems Engineering from the U.S. Naval Academy, a M.S. in Modeling, Virtual Environments and Simulation from the Naval Postgraduate School, and a M.B.A. from Kenan-Flagler Business School at the University of North Carolina.

Jon Morris brings 20 years of experience in technology management for security solutions and programs. He began his career with Citicorp’s Global Information Network and then British Telecom where he developed solutions for Advanced Intelligent Networks. From industry he moved to consulting, first with Arthur D. Little — developing the Transition Control Center for the GSA FTS program and then in 1999 to KPMG where he managed technology partnerships for the web services practice in Mountain View, CA. In 2003 Jon joined the Noblis Center for National Security and Intelligence, and developed solutions for customers in the Intel, Defense and LEA markets. In 2009, Jon joined an international security practice in Abu Dhabi, UAE where he supported Cyber, Spatial Data Infrastructure (SDI) and advanced security initiatives for counter-terrorism programs. Most recently Jon served as the Chief Information Officer for a start-up developing identity management and validation services.

Jon received his B.A. from Arizona State University, a Graduate Certificate in Network Security from George Washington University and an M.S. in Information and Telecommunications Systems from Johns Hopkins University.
